Executive Summary¶

This project presents a comprehensive natural language processing (NLP) pipeline designed to analyze customer reviews of 240 top-rated restaurants in Los Angeles. The goal of the analysis was to extract insights into sentiment polarity, emotional tone, and key thematic topics present in customer feedback. The pipeline was implemented using Python and industry-standard NLP libraries, and the methodology focused entirely on transforming raw textual data into interpretable emotional and thematic patterns.

The analysis began with a structured approach to data preparation. The dataset included review text, star ratings, restaurant names, and pricing tiers. Initial preprocessing addressed missing values in categorical and numerical fields. The core text preprocessing pipeline included contraction expansion, spelling correction using the SymSpell algorithm, named entity recognition, part-of-speech filtering, stopword customization, lemmatization, and phrase modeling. This created a clean and semantically meaningful version of the text for further analysis.

Two major components of sentiment analysis were implemented. First, TextBlob was used to assign a continuous polarity score ranging from -1 (negative) to +1 (positive) to each review. Second, emotional content was quantified using the NRC Emotion Lexicon, yielding normalized scores across ten emotions: joy, trust, anticipation, positive, surprise, sadness, fear, anger, disgust, and negative. These values provided a multidimensional emotional fingerprint for each review.

To uncover the deeper thematic structure of the reviews, Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) was used for topic modeling. Preprocessing for this step included additional part-of-speech filtering (nouns and adjectives only) and vectorization using a count-based document-term matrix. After evaluating models with 2 to 14 topics using log-likelihood and perplexity, a 5-topic model was selected for its balance of coherence and interpretability. Each review was then assigned a topic distribution, and the most dominant topic was identified and appended to the data.

The final stage of analysis focused on comparing sentiment polarity and emotional scores across topics. Average polarity scores and normalized emotion proportions were computed for each dominant topic, revealing how different thematic elements - such as food quality, service, or ambiance - were associated with specific emotional experiences. Radar and bar plots were used to visualize these relationships.

This end-to-end NLP pipeline resulted in a richly annotated dataset that links sentiment and emotion with latent themes in customer reviews. The approach enables restaurant stakeholders to interpret unstructured customer feedback through the lenses of emotional response and thematic focus, offering practical insight into what aspects of the dining experience matter most to their clientele.

Methodology¶

This study presents a multi-step natural language processing pipeline for analyzing customer reviews of restaurants in Los Angeles. The analysis focuses on extracting sentiment polarity, emotional content, and latent thematic structures to uncover patterns in customer feedback. The methodology includes data ingestion, text preprocessing, sentiment and emotion scoring, topic modeling, and sentiment analysis by topic. All steps are applied using Python and several key NLP libraries within a reproducible pipeline.

Data Collection and Initial Preparation¶

The dataset used for this analysis contains user reviews and metadata for 240 top-rated restaurants in Los Angeles. It was read into a pandas DataFrame and included variables such as restaurant name, star rating, date, price tier, and a free-text review column. Missing values in the categorical fields (RestaurantName, Price) were imputed using placeholder values. Numeric fields with missing entries were filled with zeros.

Text Preprocessing¶

To prepare the textual data for analysis, a robust preprocessing pipeline was developed with the following steps:

  • Contraction Expansion: All English contractions were expanded to their full forms using the contractions library.
  • Spelling Correction: Misspelled words were corrected using the SymSpell algorithm and a preloaded frequency dictionary.
  • Lowercasing and Cleaning: All characters were lowercased, and non-alphabetic characters were removed.
  • Named Entity Consolidation: Multi-word named entities identified by SpaCy’s NER engine were joined with underscores to preserve their semantic unity.
  • Token Filtering: Using SpaCy’s POS tagger, only nouns, adjectives, verbs, adverbs, particles, and conjunctions were retained. Custom stopword filtering preserved key sentiment-bearing words, including negators (e.g., not, never) and intensifiers (e.g., very, absolutely).
  • Lemmatization: All retained tokens were lemmatized to their base forms.
  • Phrase Modeling: Common bigrams and trigrams were identified using Gensim's Phrases and merged into single tokens.
  • Rejoining Tokens: Cleaned and tokenized text was joined back into space-separated strings for downstream modeling.

The result was stored as a new clean_text column.

Sentiment and Emotion Scoring¶

Two scoring components were implemented to quantify sentiment:

  • Polarity Scoring: TextBlob was used to assign a sentiment polarity score to each review, ranging from -1 (most negative) to +1 (most positive).
  • Emotion Scoring: The NRC Emotion Lexicon, accessed via the NRCLex library, provided normalized emotion scores for ten emotional dimensions: joy, positive, trust, fear, negative, anticipation, sadness, anger, disgust, and surprise. Emotion counts were normalized to produce proportion scores.

Both scorers were wrapped in custom scikit-learn transformers and applied sequentially using a unified pipeline. Resulting scores were appended to the main DataFrame for each review.

Categorical Feature Encoding¶

Restaurant names were encoded using scikit-learn's OrdinalEncoder to convert string labels into numeric codes. The Price field, originally containing symbols such as $ or $$$, was mapped to ordinal integers from 1 to 4. Missing entries were set to 0. This will allow us to implement predictive analytics should we wish to expand upon the analysis.

Topic Modeling via Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA)¶

To extract latent thematic topics:

  • POS Filtering for LDA: For more coherent topic modeling, a separate filtered version of the text was created (lda_clean_text). This retained only nouns and adjectives after lemmatization and removed stopwords and non-alphabetic tokens.
  • Vectorization: The filtered text was vectorized into a document-term matrix using CountVectorizer, with min_df=2, max_df=0.95, and English stopwords removed.
  • Model Selection: LDA models were trained for a range of topic counts (2-14). Log-likelihood and perplexity were computed to evaluate model fit. A 5-topic model was ultimately selected as a balance between interpretability and statistical performance.
  • Topic Assignment: Each document was assigned a vector of topic proportions, which was appended to the original DataFrame. The dominant topic per review was defined as the topic with the highest proportion.

Sentiment Analysis by Topic¶

To connect sentiment and emotional tone with underlying themes:

  • Average Polarity by Topic: Mean polarity scores were computed for each dominant topic to assess the sentiment associated with each theme.
  • Average Emotion by Topic: The same was done for each emotion dimension, yielding a detailed emotional profile for each topic.

Visualizations, including bar charts and radar plots, were generated to compare sentiment and emotion distributions across topics.

Visualization and Interpretation¶

Key plots included:

  • A radar chart summarizing average emotion levels across all reviews.
  • Bar plots comparing average polarity and emotion proportions by dominant topic.

These visualizations enabled intuitive interpretations of emotional trends and sentiment distribution tied to specific aspects of the dining experience.

This methodology produced a multi-dimensional understanding of customer feedback, uncovering both broad emotional tone and specific thematic concerns. It supports data-driven insights into how diners perceive food, service, ambiance, and other key elements across a competitive urban restaurant landscape.

Corpus Preprocessing¶

To prepare the textual data for analysis, we designed and applied a comprehensive preprocessing pipeline that addressed various forms of noise and inconsistency in the raw reviews. Our goal was to standardize the input text while preserving semantically meaningful structures and important modifiers such as negators and intensifiers.

We began with text normalization, where we first expanded contractions (e.g., “don’t” to “do not”) to their full forms. We then applied automated spelling correction using the SymSpell algorithm, which efficiently corrected common typographical errors based on a frequency dictionary. This helped reduce vocabulary sparsity caused by misspellings.

Following this, we converted all characters to lowercase and removed non-alphabetic symbols to simplify the token space. We also utilized a named entity recognition step to detect multi-word named entities (e.g., "Los Angeles") and replaced them with underscore-joined versions (e.g., "los_angeles") to treat them as single semantic units throughout the analysis.

Linguistically, we filtered tokens based on part-of-speech tagging, retaining only nouns, adjectives, verbs, adverbs, particles, and conjunctions. This helped us remove less informative words like determiners or interjections. Additionally, we implemented a custom stopword filtering strategy. Instead of discarding all common stopwords, we retained important negation terms (e.g., “not”, “never”) and positive intensifiers (e.g., “very”, “definitely”) to preserve key sentiment-bearing words often removed by default filters.

After lemmatization and token filtering, we applied a phrase modeling step to detect and consolidate frequent bigrams and trigrams (e.g., "ice cream", "chicken tikka masala") into single tokens. This ensured multi-word expressions were treated as cohesive units, improving the semantic richness of downstream analyses.

Finally, the cleaned and tokenized lists were joined back into processed text strings, ready for modeling or vectorization. This preprocessing pipeline was applied to all customer review texts in the dataset, and the resulting clean texts were stored in a new column for subsequent analysis.

def load_spacy_model(model_name: str = "en_core_web_sm") -> spacy.language.Language:
    """
    Load the SpaCy NLP model, downloading it if needed.

    try:

class SpellingCorrector:
    """
    Corrects spelling errors using the SymSpell algorithm.
    """

    def __init__(self, dictionary_path: str = "data/frequency_dictionary_en_82_765.txt") -> None:
        self.sym_spell = SymSpell(max_dictionary_edit_distance=2, prefix_length=7)
        if not os.path.exists(dictionary_path):
            os.makedirs("data", exist_ok=True)
            url = (
                "https://raw.githubusercontent.com/mammothb/symspellpy/master/"
                "symspellpy/frequency_dictionary_en_82_765.txt"
            )
            r = requests.get(url)
            with open(dictionary_path, "wb") as f:
                f.write(r.content)
        self.sym_spell.load_dictionary(dictionary_path, 0, 1)

    def correct_text(self, text: str) -> str:
        """
        Apply spelling correction to each word in the input string.
        """
        words = text.split()
        corrected = []
        for word in words:
            suggestions = self.sym_spell.lookup(word, Verbosity.CLOSEST, max_edit_distance=2)
            corrected.append(suggestions[0].term if suggestions else word)
        return " ".join(corrected)


class TextPreprocessor(BaseEstimator, TransformerMixin):
    """
    Applies text normalization, spelling correction, named entity grouping, 
    lemmatization, and stopword filtering.
    """

    def __init__(self, negators: Optional[set] = None, intensifiers: Optional[set] = None) -> None:
        self.negators = negators or {
            "not", "no", "nor", "never", "none", "nothing", "nowhere", "neither",
            "without", "barely", "hardly", "scarcely", "rarely", "seldom",
        }
        self.intensifiers = intensifiers or {
            "always", "definitely", "absolutely", "certainly", "clearly", "indeed",
            "surely", "truly", "very", "really", "extremely", "completely", "totally",
            "highly", "greatly", "positively", "successfully", "frequently", "often",
        }
        self.custom_stopwords = nlp.Defaults.stop_words \
            .difference(self.negators).difference(self.intensifiers)
        self.allowed_pos = {"NOUN", "ADJ", "VERB", "ADV", "PART", "CONJ"}
        self.corrector = SpellingCorrector()

    def fit(self, X, y=None):
        return self

    def transform(self, X: pd.Series) -> pd.Series:
        """
        Preprocess each document in the input series.
        """
        tqdm.pandas(desc="Preprocessing")
        return X.progress_apply(self._process)

    def _process(self, text: str) -> List[str]:
        """
        Clean, normalize, and tokenize input text.
        """
        try:
            if pd.isnull(text):
                return []

            text = contractions.fix(str(text))
            text = self.corrector.correct_text(text)
            text = text.lower()
            text = re.sub(r"[^a-z\s]", "", text)

            doc = nlp(text)

            ent_tokens = {
                ent.text.lower(): ent.text.lower().replace(" ", "_")
                for ent in doc.ents if len(ent.text.split()) > 1
            }

            modified_text = text
            for original, underscored in ent_tokens.items():
                modified_text = modified_text.replace(original, underscored)

            doc = nlp(modified_text)

            tokens = []
            for token in doc:
                t = token.text.lower()
                if token.is_space:
                    continue
                if t in self.negators or t in self.intensifiers:
                    tokens.append(t)
                elif token.pos_ in self.allowed_pos and t not in self.custom_stopwords:
                    tokens.append(token.lemma_)

            return tokens

        except Exception as e:
            print(f"Error on: {text[:30]}... => {e}")
            return []


class PhraseModeler(BaseEstimator, TransformerMixin):
    """
    Detects and merges common bigrams and trigrams from tokenized documents.
    """

    def __init__(self, min_count: int = 5, threshold: int = 10) -> None:
        self.min_count = min_count
        self.threshold = threshold

    def fit(self, X: List[List[str]], y=None):
        self.bigram = Phrases(X, min_count=self.min_count, threshold=self.threshold)
        self.bigram_mod = Phraser(self.bigram)
        self.trigram = Phrases([self.bigram_mod[doc] for doc in X], threshold=self.threshold)
        self.trigram_mod = Phraser(self.trigram)
        return self

    def transform(self, X: List[List[str]]) -> List[List[str]]:
        """
        Apply bigram and trigram modeling to the input token lists.
        """
        return [self.trigram_mod[self.bigram_mod[doc]] for doc in X]


class TextJoiner(BaseEstimator, TransformerMixin):
    """
    Joins tokenized lists of words into space-separated strings.
    """

    def fit(self, X, y=None):
        return self

    def transform(self, X: List[List[str]]) -> List[str]:
        return [" ".join(doc) for doc in X]

Sentiment Scoring¶

In this section, we developed a robust sentiment scoring framework to quantify both general sentiment polarity and nuanced emotional content within the customer reviews.

First, we implemented a PolarityScorer transformer that computes a polarity score for each review using TextBlob’s sentiment analyzer. This polarity score ranges from -1 (most negative) to +1 (most positive), providing a simple scalar measure of overall sentiment for each text entry.

To capture a richer emotional landscape, we built an EmotionScorer transformer leveraging the NRC Emotion Lexicon through the NRCLex library. For each review, this component extracts counts of eight basic emotions (such as joy, anger, sadness, fear) and normalizes them by the total detected emotion count, resulting in proportional emotion intensity scores. These emotion scores offer a multidimensional view of the affective states expressed in the text beyond polarity alone.

Both scorers were integrated into a single scikit-learn pipeline for streamlined processing. We applied this pipeline to the preprocessed review texts, appending the resulting polarity and emotion features to the dataset.

Additionally, categorical metadata fields - such as restaurant names and price tiers - were prepared for analysis by filling missing values and encoding them numerically. This preprocessing ensures these important contextual variables can be effectively incorporated into downstream modeling tasks.

Finally, missing numeric values were imputed with zeros to maintain dataset integrity. The processed dataset now contains sentiment polarity, detailed emotion proportions, and relevant encoded metadata, providing a comprehensive foundation for sentiment-based analysis and predictive modeling.

class PolarityScorer(BaseEstimator, TransformerMixin):
    """
    Transformer that computes sentiment polarity scores for text data.
    """

    def fit(self, X: pd.DataFrame, y: Optional[pd.Series] = None) -> "PolarityScorer":
        """
        Fit method does nothing and returns self.
        """
        return self

    def transform(self, X: pd.DataFrame) -> pd.DataFrame:
        """
        Compute polarity score for each text entry in 'clean_text' column.
        """
        X = X.copy()
        tqdm.pandas(desc="Scoring Polarity")
        X["polarity"] = X["clean_text"].progress_apply(
            lambda text: TextBlob(text).sentiment.polarity if isinstance(text, str) else 0.0
        )
        return X


class EmotionScorer(BaseEstimator, TransformerMixin):
    """
    Transformer that computes normalized emotion scores for text data.
    """

    def fit(self, X: pd.DataFrame, y: Optional[pd.Series] = None) -> "EmotionScorer":
        """
        Fit method does nothing and returns self.
        """
        return self

    def transform(self, X: pd.DataFrame) -> pd.DataFrame:
        """
        Compute normalized emotion scores for each text entry in 'clean_text' column
        and merge the resulting emotion columns into the dataframe.
        """
        X = X.copy()
        tqdm.pandas(desc="Scoring Emotions")
        emotion_dicts = X["clean_text"].progress_apply(self._score_emotions)
        emotion_df = pd.json_normalize(emotion_dicts)
        emotion_df.columns = [f"emotion_{col}" for col in emotion_df.columns]
        emotion_df.index = X.index
        X = pd.concat([X, emotion_df], axis=1)
        return X

    def _score_emotions(self, text: Union[str, object]) -> Dict[str, float]:
        """
        Return normalized emotion scores extracted from the text.
        """
        try:
            if not isinstance(text, str) or not text.strip():
                return {}
            emotions = NRCLex(text).raw_emotion_scores
            total = sum(emotions.values())
            if total:
                return {k: v / total for k, v in emotions.items()}
            return {}
        except Exception:
            return {}

Topic Modeling¶

Before performing Latent Dirichlet Allocation for topic modeling, we added an additional preprocessing stage that filters the text to keep only specific parts of speech - namely nouns and adjectives - while removing stopwords and non-alphabetic tokens. While the broader set of parts of speech used earlier was effective for sentiment scoring, including verbs and other categories would introduce noise for LDA topic modeling and degrade the quality of the topics. This targeted filtering helps reduce noise and retain only the most meaningful words for LDA, improving the clarity and coherence of the resulting topics.

def filter_for_lda(text: str) -> str:
    """
    Keep only nouns and adjectives from the text after lemmatizing.
    Removes stop words and non-alphabetic tokens.
    """
    doc = nlp(text)
    keep_pos: Set[str] = {"NOUN", "ADJ"}
    filtered_tokens = [
        token.lemma_ for token in doc
        if token.pos_ in keep_pos and not token.is_stop and token.is_alpha
    ]
    return " ".join(filtered_tokens)

def evaluate_lda_models(dtm: pd.Series, topic_range: range) -> Tuple[List[float], List[float]]:
    """
    Fits LDA models for a range of topic numbers and returns the log likelihoods
    and perplexities for each.
    """
    log_likelihoods = []
    perplexities = []

    for n_topics in topic_range:
        lda = LatentDirichletAllocation(n_components=n_topics, random_state=42)
        lda.fit(dtm)
        log_likelihoods.append(lda.score(dtm))
        perplexities.append(lda.perplexity(dtm))

    return log_likelihoods, perplexities

Business Recommendations¶

As part of our review of customer sentiment, emotional tone, and thematic content across a wide range of restaurant reviews in Los Angeles, we identified consistent patterns in what diners value most - and what tends to frustrate them. The analysis spans thousands of reviews and was conducted to help you, our client, position your own restaurant competitively within the local market by learning from shared industry strengths and pain points.

Enhance the Dining Experience¶

Across all restaurants analyzed, the theme related to the dining experience exhibited strong emotional positivity. This topic had a mean polarity score of 0.34 and consistently elicited joy (0.181), trust (0.169), and positivity (0.306). It also maintained low levels of negative emotions such as fear (0.024) and anger (0.023). These findings suggest that diners strongly associate satisfaction with the holistic experience - ranging from ambiance and service to presentation and pacing. Your restaurant should view this as a core competitive pillar. Elevating or even maintaining strong performance in this area will require ongoing investments in staff consistency, seamless guest flow, and creating a “complete” dining atmosphere. This is the area most frequently mentioned in 5-star reviews across the LA market, underscoring its value as a differentiator.

Improve Order and Table Service¶

Order- and table-related themes showed lower sentiment performance, with a mean polarity score of 0.17, and elevated levels of negative emotions, particularly anger (0.048), sadness (0.034), and fear (0.038). These reviews often referenced delayed service, forgotten items, and inconsistent staff attentiveness. This was one of the most common sources of customer dissatisfaction, appearing more frequently in 3-star and below reviews. Given this, your restaurant can gain a competitive edge by minimizing these friction points. Prioritizing streamlined service workflows and ensuring that each table receives consistent, timely attention can transform these moments of weakness into strengths. In a market where diners often return to establishments based on reliability, addressing this area proactively could substantially improve your long-term rating trajectory.

Promote High-Quality Food¶

Food quality continues to be a major driver of customer satisfaction, as reflected in this topic’s mean polarity of 0.40 - one of the highest among all themes. Positive emotions were strong across the board, including joy (0.199), trust (0.170), and positivity (0.324). The topic often included phrases like "fresh," "delicious," and "flavorful." Restaurants in LA that scored highest on these attributes were also those with a disproportionate number of 4.5 to 5-star reviews. Your restaurant should ensure that ingredient sourcing, consistency in preparation, and quality control remain top priorities. Furthermore, sharing behind-the-scenes content - like chef spotlights or supplier partnerships - can build emotional trust, reinforcing this as a standout feature for your brand.

Leverage Ambiance as a Competitive Advantage¶

The topic related to restaurant atmosphere emerged as the strongest across both polarity and emotion dimensions. It had the highest mean polarity (0.48) and was particularly rich in joy (0.213) and trust (0.186). Customers consistently responded positively to well-curated ambiance, from lighting and music to decor and seating layouts. This finding suggests that visual and sensory design is not just aesthetic - it directly impacts perceived value and emotional engagement. Among top-performing restaurants, ambiance was cited as a reason for repeat visits nearly as often as food quality. Your restaurant should evaluate how your space is contributing to emotional tone and consider seasonal or event-based ambiance upgrades as part of your brand narrative.

Refine Flavor Profiles and Seasoning¶

The flavor-focused topic showed more variability in customer feedback. While the average polarity was moderately positive at 0.26, negative emotion levels - particularly disgust (0.033) and anger (0.031) - were noticeably elevated compared to other topics. This suggests that, while many diners appreciate bold or unique flavor combinations, there is also a higher risk of dissatisfaction when flavor execution falls short. Restaurants that succeeded in this domain often allowed customization of spice levels or emphasized familiar flavor anchors while experimenting more subtly. Your menu development strategy should take this into account - ideally testing new dishes via small-batch offerings or customer pilot feedback before making full-scale rollouts. Ensuring a consistently favorable flavor experience can directly support a more predictable emotional response and more frequent high ratings.

Prioritize Emotionally Resonant Touchpoints¶

Across all topics and restaurants, the most influential emotions driving positive ratings were joy, trust, and positivity, while anger, fear, and sadness dominated lower-star reviews. For instance, joy was present at a mean score of 0.181 in high-polarity topics and fell below 0.12 in low-polarity themes. Similarly, anger levels doubled from 0.023 to 0.048 between top-rated and poorly rated themes. This highlights the importance of micro-moments that shape emotional tone. Greeting guests warmly, resolving issues quickly, and offering thoughtful farewells can all elevate trust and joy. Your restaurant should actively manage these moments as part of the service blueprint, using them to foster emotionally resonant experiences that translate into higher loyalty and better ratings.

The strongest relationships between topic and star rating were found in themes related to restaurant ambiance and food quality.
